I first came across Founders & Coders last summer and was thrilled to discover the programme’s unique approach. After switching to a part-time position recently, I dedicated the past four months to completing the course prerequisites and I am excited to finally submit my application for the Summer 2019 Cohort.

Experience so far

For the past five years, I have been Digital Manager at NVS, a record label and music company organising three music festivals - Outlook, Dimensions and We Out Here. In this role, I oversee all aspects of the company’s digital output, commission and edit content and use social media to reach musical communities around Europe. I also run programmatic advertising campaigns and work closely with our web developer to update the company’s websites.

After a Bachelor’s in Philosophy, Politics and Economics, I completed a Master’s in London and wrote a dissertation on the changing models of independent record labels in the digital economy, before pursuing work in the music industry.

I have thoroughly enjoyed working in this creative environment and have felt privileged to collaborate with dedicated colleagues and talented artists, but, for a while now, I have been thinking about changing paths to broaden my horizons and work on socially-driven projects with collaboration at their heart.

Learning how to code

I was intrigued by coding for a while before getting started. I was eager to better understand the platforms which, for better or worse, play such an important role in shaping our everyday life through our phones, tablets and laptops. I met with programmers to learn more about their job and discovered some of them worked on impact-led initiatives related to social mobility, investigative journalism and other social enterprises.

Last summer, I started using Founders & Coders’ prerequisites as a learning template before attending an information meeting and coding meet-ups in the autumn. The programme’s people, ethos and learning approach made me feel motivated and convinced me that it would be the ideal community to become a fully-fledged developer.

Joining Founders & Coders

Peer-to-peer learning happened organically in my digital marketing job. I trained and managed new starters in the digital team, shared digital insights with the wider team weekly, and became versed in graphic design and video editing thanks to the time and goodwill of my co-workers. This collaborative learning approach seems particularly suited to coding, where each individual confronted with the same problem is likely to come up with its own particular solution.

Founders & Coders’ commitment to supporting impactful initiatives through the Tech For Better programme is also inspiring and something I would love to be a part of. After completing the programme, I would be pleased to give back to the community – and put my new knowledge to the test – by mentoring new students and prospective applicants.
